##  Airbnb NYC Data Analysis Using Python: Pricing & Demand Insights

---

### 🔹 Project Overview

This project performs exploratory data analysis (EDA) on the Airbnb NYC 2019 dataset to identify key factors influencing pricing, customer preferences, and listing performance. The goal is to extract meaningful insights and provide data-driven recommendations.

---

### 🔹 Problem Statement

In a competitive short-term rental market, understanding pricing dynamics and customer behavior is essential. This project analyzes Airbnb listings to uncover trends that can help optimize pricing strategies and improve occupancy.

---

### 🔹 Dataset

* **Source:** Airbnb NYC 2019 dataset
* **Size:** ~48,000 listings
* **Key Features:**

  * Price
  * Room Type
  * Neighborhood Group
  * Availability (365 days)
  * Number of Reviews
  * Host Information

---

### 🔹 Tools & Technologies

* Python
* Pandas, NumPy
* Matplotlib, Seaborn
* Plotly
* Jupyter Notebook

---

### 🔹 Key Insights

* Manhattan listings have significantly higher average prices (~2x compared to other areas)
* Entire homes dominate the market (~50–60% of listings), indicating strong customer preference
* Weak correlation between price and number of reviews
* Over 30% of listings show high availability, suggesting possible low demand or overpricing

---

### 🔹 Business Recommendations

* Apply dynamic pricing strategies in high-demand neighborhoods
* Focus on entire home listings to maximize revenue
* Improve visibility of low-review listings through competitive pricing
* Optimize pricing for high-availability properties to increase occupancy

---

### 🔹 Conclusion

The analysis highlights how location, room type, and availability influence Airbnb pricing and demand. These insights can help hosts and platforms make informed, data-driven decisions.

---

### 🔹 Limitations

This dataset is from 2019 and is used for analytical learning purposes. Market conditions may have changed post-pandemic, but the analytical approach remains relevant.
